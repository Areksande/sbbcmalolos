TESTED, YET FAITHFUL

Job 2:9-10 KJV
9 Then said his wife unto him, Dost thou still retain thine integrity? curse God, and die.
10 But he said unto her, Thou speakest as one of the foolish women speaketh. What? shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil? In all this did not Job sin with his lips.
God gives us both a gift and a test. A new season does not only bring new opportunities it also reveals our integrity, our honesty, and our commitment to walk in God’s will. As we step into this new season the Lord has given us, the question is not only where God is leading us, but how we will walk with Him.
The Bible reminds us that walking in God’s will requires more than words it requires a life anchored in integrity and strengthened by commitment.

THE PRESSURE OF A NEW SEASON

Every new season tests our integrity.

The Pain of the Season

Job experienced deep loss his family, his livelihood, and his health were taken from him. New seasons often come after loss, change, or shaking. God allows pressure not to destroy us, but to reveal what is within us.

The Persuasion in the Season

Job’s wife urged him to abandon his integrity: “Curse God, and die.” In seasons of pressure, voices will rise that encourage compromise, bitterness, or withdrawal from God.

The Position of Faith in the Season

Job stood firm. He remembered God’s goodness and declared, “Shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil?” In all this, Job did not sin with his lips. Integrity is choosing faithfulness even when circumstances are unfavorable.

THE PRINCIPLES THAT SUSTAIN THE SEASON

Integrity is built on truth and honesty.

Honesty Builds Character

Faithfulness in small things develops godly character. Honest stewardship invites greater responsibility from God.

Honesty Brings Maturity

Spiritual growth happens when truth is spoken in love. Honesty before God and others helps us grow into Christ.

Honesty Brings Security

The upright are delivered by righteousness. A life of honesty brings peace, protection, and stability. When trust is high, influence is high; when trust is low, influence is low.

THE PROMISES WE STRENGTHEN IN THE SEASON

Walking in God’s will requires faithful commitment.

Commitment Aligns Our Walk

As we received Christ in faith, so must we continue walking in obedience. Commitment keeps our steps aligned with God’s will.

Commitment Anchors the Heart

Giving God our heart includes giving Him our word. Our promises reflect the depth of our devotion.

Commitment Honors the Lord

God takes our vows seriously.Respecting God means remembering and fulfilling what we promised Him.

THE PRACTICES THAT SUPPORT THE SEASON

The Greatest Christmas Response Obedience in practical areas keeps us walking in His will.

Faithful Giving Is Biblical

Tithes, increase, firstfruits, and sacrificial giving are clearly taught in Scripture.

Faithful Giving Breaks Selfishness

Giving guards our hearts from covetousness and self-centered living.

Faithful Giving Brings God’s Favor

We have practiced it, and we have experienced God’s faithfulness.

Faithful Giving Builds Our Future

God still has greater blessings ahead. Giving helps us fulfill our commitments and reach our God-given potential.

Conclusion:

A PRAYERFUL RESPONSE FOR A NEW SEASON
As we enter this new season, the Lord calls us to:
* Stand firm in integrity
* Speak and live in honesty
* Strengthen our commitments
* Step forward in obedience
