I. DEFINING CHURCH MINISTRIES
A. The “WHAT” of church Ministries
Ministry is not what you can get but what you can do for Christ. Ministry is not a job or a Sunday routine, it’s a privilege to be chosen and used by Jesus.

1. The Goal of Church Ministries.
Ephesians 4:12-13
12 For the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ: 13 Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God, unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fulness of Christ:

- Church Ministries are all about equipping and furnishing [perfecting],
- Attendance to serving [work of the ministry],
- and growing and building up [edifying the body] until the whole body is completely measured up to the character and the person of Jesus Christ (may grow up into Him in all things).

Its platform of government is humble service. Its desire is to please the Lord,

the Head of the Church: - Its attitude is obedience to His will;
- Its motive is “By love, serve one another,”
- Its goal is to glorify God;
- Its mandate is the Great Commission;

and Its three most important purpose are:
-EVANGELISM (Soul-Winning)
-EDUCATION (Discipleship); and
-EDIFICATION (Worship)

2. The Premise of Church Ministries, The Church is:
i. RUN LIKE BUSINESS;
Running a business takes hard work, dedication, and commitment. Whether you’re just starting out on your own or have been running a business for a while, there are a few things you can do to set yourself and your business up for success

Legend:
> Business
> Ministry
> Hire the right team.
> in the ministry:
Pray, love, and teach that we may produce the right team

Success depends on having the right people in the right roles. The key is to build your team so that every person is working in their area of expertise. There’s no one-size-fits-all solution, so you have to identify your business or ministry’s specific needs. Think about the areas that need the most attention and whether bringing in a dedicated team member will help improve things overall.

> Have a personal advisory board.
> in the ministry You need:
-a model to inspire us
-a mentor to coach us
-a partner to assist us &
-a friend who brings out the best in us!

Especially in the early stages of business or ministry, you need people who you can trust to turn to for advice. Your personal advisory board can be made up of mentors, fellow worker or staff from other churches, and in select cases, family members.

>Perseverance is everything.
> in the ministry you need to have faith

In any business or ministry, there are many trials and tribulations amongst the successes. The key is to not let things get you down, but to instead take those challenges and use them to fuel your drive and desire. It takes perseverance to push through the challenges to come out on the other side and achieve success.

>Create a Business Plan
> in the ministry we have Mission and Vision, short term and long term goal.

Starting and growing a business or ministry is a complicated affair. Don't try to wing it. Create a plan to lay out your operations for the next few years and the steps you need to take to achieve your goals.
The plan will help keep you organized.
> Don't stop evolving
> in the ministry don’t stop learning and improving

Your strategy, your plan, your target achievements nothing is set in stone. The world is changing more and more rapidly each day. The best way to remain relevant is to keep your eyes open for changing tides, your mind open to new ideas and your skills in flexibility top-notch.

Notice: be careful for negotiable and non negotiable.

B. The “WHY” of the Church Ministries.
The Lord built His Church to be:
1. The Repository of divine truth:
2. The Recruitment agency for God’s kingdom;
3. The Responsible agency to carry out the divine mandate;
4. The Responding institution
to the helpless and hopeless;
5. The Revolutionary army for divine change; and
6. The Representative earthly institution of the heavenly kingdom.

C. The ‘HOW’ of the Church Ministries.
1. Methods taught by the Lord Jesus Christ Himself and the apostles;
2. Pastoral teachings and experiences; and
3. Scriptural methods and promotions.

D. The “WHEN” of Church Ministries.
The Church started during the personal earthly ministry of the Lord Jesus Christ and it will have its conclusion at the time of the Rapture.

The Church at present is now marching toward her Stability, Continuity and Maturity (Matthew 16:15-18)

E. The ‘WHO’ of the Church Ministries.
God put men and women into Church Ministries to serve all His divine purpose. He equips and prepares them with Godly virtues such as:

1. Maturity (1 Cor. 14:20; Ephesians 4:14-15; 1 Cor. 13:11)
2. Faithfulness (Luke 16:10-12; 2 Cor. 5:7; Det. 28)
3. Loyalty (1 Cor. 4:2; Proverbs 17:17)
4. Humility (James 4:6; Phil. 2:3-11; Luke 14:11) and
5. Efficiency (Eph. 5:15-17; Luke 14:28; Psalms 90:12)

APPLICATION:
The ministries as church composite are designed to provide opportunities and avenues for spiritual growth and maturity of every believer.
If you are serving the Lord faithfully in one or two ministries here in our church, I want you to know that they can be your source of joy, strength, honor and fulfillment.
And every faithful service that you do for the Lord will surely be rewarded.
It’s all worth serving God in loving obedience and response to what Christ has done for you and me.
The Lord says that His Father will honor the one who serves Him
1 Peter 4:10 As every man hath received the gift, even so minister the same one to another, as good stewards of the manifold grace of God.

Ephesians 3:21 Unto him be glory in the church by Christ Jesus throughout all ages, world without end. Amen.

1 Corinthians 15:58 Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye stedfast, unmoveable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord.

John 12:26 If any man serve me, let him follow me; and where I am, there shall also my servant be: if any man serve me, him will my Father honour.
